### Observations

* **Research‑to‑live parity**: QuantConnect’s LEAN and Backtrader emphasise modular design where the same algorithm can backtest and execute live, providing high parity.  VectorBT prioritises vectorized speed but includes event‑driven callbacks to approximate live behaviour.  Freqtrade offers dry‑run simulation but is limited to crypto markets.
* **Event semantics**: Backtrader explicitly states that trading logic and broker interactions are always run **event by event**【128527540200510†L291-L299】.  VectorBT supports event‑driven callbacks【160109905961433†L510-L518】.  In Freqtrade, dry‑run mode simulates order events and slippage but may diverge from actual exchange behaviour【273988189869681†L1545-L1583】.
* **Gaps and opening auctions**: None of the surveyed tools provide dedicated gap‑classification modules or opening auction analysis.  They all treat price data generically and leave interpretation up to the user.  This gap in the market motivates **GapLab**: an opening reaction laboratory that classifies gaps by catalyst, structure, liquidity and expected path without automating trades.
